## Wash Sale Rules

### The 61-Day Window

```
       <-------- 30 days --------|-------- 30 days -------->
Wash Sale Window: [Sale Date - 30] to [Sale Date + 30]

If you buy "substantially identical" security within this window:
- Loss is DISALLOWED for tax purposes
- Disallowed loss added to cost basis of new shares
- Holding period of original shares carries forward
```

### Substantially Identical

**IS wash sale**:
- Same stock (AAPL → AAPL)
- Stock and call option on same stock
- Mutual fund and ETF tracking same index

**NOT wash sale**:
- Different companies in same sector (AAPL → MSFT)
- Different index funds (S&P 500 → Total Market)
- Stock and a different company's stock

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Calendar

### Optimal Harvest Times

| Period | Strategy |
|--------|----------|
| November-December | Year-end harvesting before December 31 |
| After market drops | Harvest losses during corrections |
| After earnings miss | Harvest losers while fundamentals unclear |
| 31+ days before year-end | Allow wash sale window to clear |

### Harvest Decision Matrix

| Loss Amount | Days to Year-End | Action |
|-------------|------------------|--------|
| >$3,000 | >31 days | Harvest now |
| >$3,000 | <31 days | Harvest, but don't buy back this year |
| <$3,000 | Any | Carry forward, consider harvesting |
| Any | >31 days | Can replace with similar (not identical) |

## Red Flags Taxley Watches For

### Imminent Long-Term Threshold
**Signal**: Position at gain, 10-30 days from 1-year holding
**Response**: "This position goes long-term in 22 days. Selling now costs an extra 22% in taxes. Recommend waiting."

### Wash Sale Risk
**Signal**: Recent sale at loss, considering repurchase
**Response**: "You sold this at a loss 18 days ago. Buying back would disallow that loss. Wait until day 31 or buy a similar but not identical security."

### Year-End Loss Opportunity
**Signal**: Unrealized losses in November/December
**Response**: "You have $8,000 in unrealized losses. Harvesting now offsets gains and provides $3,000 deduction against ordinary income."
