## Two-Tiered Affiliate Structure

**Concept:** Create two parallel affiliate programsâ€”one for money, one for non-monetary incentives.

### Tier 1: Money-Based Affiliates

**Who:** Professional marketers, influencers with large audiences, agencies
**Incentive:** Cash commission per sale
**Typical Commission:** 30-50% of sale price
**Why They Promote:** Direct financial gain

**Example from $100M Money Models:**
- $40 book price
- $12-20 commission per sale
- Large affiliates made $50K-$500K+

### Tier 2: Non-Monetary Affiliates

**Who:** Customers, fans, small audiences, mission-aligned partners
**Incentive:** Recognition, status, exclusive access, impact
**Typical Rewards:**
- Leaderboard rankings
- Exclusive content
- Direct access to you
- Public recognition
- Community status

**Example from $100M Money Models:**
- Top promoter leaderboard
- Behind-the-scenes access
- Recognition in launch content
- Community leadership roles

**Why This Works:**

Some people are motivated by money, others by status/mission. Two tiers = capture both groups.

## Affiliate Offer Construction

**Framework:** Decide if your affiliate program is money-based or mission-based, then structure accordingly.

### Money vs. Non-Monetary Decision Tree

**Choose MONEY-BASED if:**
- High ticket product ($500+)
- Professional marketer audience
- You have profit margin for commissions
- Need immediate scale

**Choose NON-MONETARY if:**
- Low ticket product (<$100)
- Customer/fan audience
- Tight profit margins
- Building long-term community

**Choose BOTH if:**
- You want maximum reach
- Can afford commission structure
- Have mission-aligned message
- Want both pro marketers AND grassroots promotion

**Hormozi's Choice:** BOTH (money for pros, recognition for fans)

## Affiliate Support System (Three Pillars)

**Concept:** Affiliates need support in three areas to succeed.

### Pillar 1: Collateral (Marketing Materials)

**Provide:**
- Pre-written emails (3-5 variations)
- Social media posts (10-20 ready-to-post)
- Graphics and images (square, story, landscape formats)
- Video clips (15-60 seconds)
- Blog post templates
- Sales scripts

**Key:** Remove ALL friction. They should copy-paste and go.

### Pillar 2: Tracking (Attribution System)

**Provide:**
- Unique affiliate links
- Real-time dashboard
- Sales tracking
- Commission tracking
- Leaderboard (if using gamification)

**Technology:**
- Affiliate tracking software
- Automated commission calculations
- Transparent reporting

### Pillar 3: Communication (Ongoing Updates)

**Provide:**
- Launch timeline
- Regular email updates
- Slack/Discord channel
- Live Q&A sessions
- Performance tips
- Winner spotlights

**Frequency:** At least 2-3 touchpoints per week during campaign

## Affiliate Activation Metrics by Channel

**Concept:** Different channels have different activation rates. Plan accordingly.

### Email Lists

**Expected Activation:** 30-50% of affiliates with email lists will promote
**Why:** Low effort, they already email audience regularly
**Support Needed:** Email templates, subject line variations

### Social Media

**Expected Activation:** 20-40% of social media affiliates will promote
**Why:** Must fit their content style, requires more customization
**Support Needed:** Platform-specific content (IG stories, TikTok format, Twitter threads)

### YouTube/Podcast

**Expected Activation:** 10-30% will promote
**Why:** Higher production effort, must fit their content calendar
**Support Needed:** Talking points, video clips, ad read scripts

### Blog/Content Sites

**Expected Activation:** 40-60% will promote
**Why:** Evergreen content, easy to add affiliate links
**Support Needed:** Blog post templates, SEO keywords, embed codes

**Planning:** If you recruit 100 affiliates with email lists, expect 30-50 to actually promote. Recruit accordingly.

## Core Four Applied to Affiliate Acquisition

**Concept:** Use your own Core Four framework to recruit affiliates.

### Warm Outreach (Recruiting Affiliates)

**Who to Contact:**
- Existing customers (they already love you)
- People who engage with your content
- Past collaborators
- Industry peers

**Message:**
"You've been a fan of my work. I'm launching [product] and would love your help spreading it. Interested in partnering?"

### Cold Outreach (Recruiting Affiliates)

**Who to Contact:**
- Influencers in your niche
- Complementary business owners
- Professional affiliates/marketers

**Message:**
"I follow your work on [topic]. I'm launching [product] that your audience would love. Would you be open to a partnership where you earn [commission]?"

### Free Content (Attracting Affiliates)

**Strategy:**
- Create "Affiliate opportunity" content
- Post about launch publicly
- Share affiliate success stories
- Make the opportunity visible

**Where:**
- Social posts about affiliate program
- YouTube video about partnership opportunity
- Blog post with affiliate signup

### Paid Ads (Attracting Affiliates)

**Strategy:**
- Run ads targeting "affiliate marketers"
- Run ads targeting "[your topic] influencers"
- Retarget your existing audience with affiliate opportunity

**Ad Message:**
"Want to earn promoting products you believe in? Join my affiliate program."

## Affiliate Terminology Mapping (Industry-Specific)

**Concept:** Different industries call affiliates different things. Use the right term.

**Affiliate Marketing =**
- "Partners" (business/B2B)
- "Ambassadors" (consumer brands)
- "Collaborators" (creative industries)
- "Promoters" (events/launches)
- "Referral partners" (professional services)

**Commission =**
- "Partnership fee" (sounds more professional)
- "Revenue share" (equity-like framing)
- "Referral bonus" (lighter weight)

**Why This Matters:** Some audiences are turned off by "affiliate marketing" language. Reframe for your market.

## Win-Win-Win Principle

**Concept:** Structure everything so all three parties benefit.

### Customer Wins

**What They Get:**
- Valuable product at fair price
- Bonuses (if available)
- Trusted recommendation from affiliate they follow
- Solution to their problem

### Affiliate Wins

**What They Get:**
- Commission/recognition
- Value to provide their audience
- Strengthened relationship with audience (helped them)
- Low effort (you provide all materials)

### You Win

**What You Get:**
- Massive distribution
- Sales/revenue
- Social proof (affiliates endorsing you)
- Long-term affiliate relationships

**Key:** If any party doesn't win, the system breaks. Design for all three.

## Leaderboard Gamification Strategy

**Concept:** Create public leaderboard for affiliate rankings to drive competition.

### Leaderboard Structure

**Tracked Metrics:**
- Total sales generated
- Total revenue generated
- Number of unique customers
- Engagement rate

**Display:**
- Real-time leaderboard (live updates)
- Public rankings (top 10-20 visible)
- Badges/tiers (Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum)

### Prize Structure

**Prizes for Top Performers:**
- Cash bonuses ($1K-$100K+ depending on scale)
- Exclusive experiences (dinner with you, mastermind access)
- Public recognition (featured in content)
- Lifetime status (VIP in community)

**Example from $100M Money Models:**
- Top affiliate earned $500K+ in commissions
- Top 10 got exclusive dinner with Hormozi
- Top 100 got featured in launch recap content

### Psychological Drivers

Leaderboard triggers:
- **Competition** - "I want to beat [person above me]"
- **Recognition** - "I want to be in top 10"
- **FOMO** - "I'm falling behind, need to push harder"
- **Status** - "Being #1 proves I'm the best"

## Affiliate Program Architecture/Design Process

**Step-by-step process to build your affiliate program:**

### Step 1: Define Your Goal

**Questions:**
- How many sales do you want affiliates to generate?
- What percentage of total sales should come from affiliates?
- Are you optimizing for volume or quality?

### Step 2: Choose Your Structure

**Decide:**
- Money-based, non-monetary, or both?
- Commission percentage (30-50% standard)
- Cookie duration (30-90 days standard)
- Payment terms (monthly, per-sale, milestone-based)

### Step 3: Create Affiliate Sales Page

**Build:**
- Landing page that sells the opportunity
- Clear value proposition for affiliates
- Sign-up process
- Terms and conditions

### Step 4: Build Support System

**Set Up:**
- Tracking technology (affiliate software)
- Collateral library (emails, posts, graphics)
- Communication channel (email, Slack, Discord)
- Dashboard for affiliates

### Step 5: Create Collateral

**Produce:**
- Base collateral (emails, posts, graphics)
- Platform-specific content (IG, TikTok, YouTube, etc.)
- Scripts and talking points
- Promotional calendar

### Step 6: Recruit Affiliates

**Use Core Four:**
- Warm outreach to customers/fans
- Cold outreach to influencers
- Free content announcing program
- Paid ads targeting affiliates

### Step 7: Launch Campaign

**Execute:**
- Onboard affiliates (welcome email, training)
- Send launch calendar
- Provide all collateral
- Go live with campaign

### Step 8: Support During Campaign

**Maintain:**
- Daily/weekly emails with updates
- Spotlight top performers
- Provide additional collateral as needed
- Answer questions in real-time

### Step 9: Reward Winners

**Deliver:**
- Pay commissions on time
- Recognize top performers publicly
- Create case studies from winners
- Provide extra support to top 10%

### Step 10: Debrief and Improve

**Analyze:**
- What worked/didn't work?
- Which channels performed best?
- Which affiliates drove most sales?
- What collateral was most used?
- Improve for next campaign

## Quick Reference Guide

**When to Use Affiliate Marketing:**
- Launching new product
- Want explosive growth
- Have compelling offer
- Can support partners
- Need distribution at scale

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Offer worth promoting
- Ability to track sales
- Budget for commissions OR non-monetary rewards
- Time to support affiliates
- Marketing collateral to provide

**Success Factors:**
1. Win-Win-Win structure (all parties benefit)
2. Remove ALL friction (provide everything needed)
3. Support affiliates throughout campaign
4. Reward top performers generously
5. Be transparent about process and results

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Poor commission structure (too low)
- Lack of collateral (affiliates don't know what to say)
- No tracking system (can't attribute sales)
- Ignoring affiliates mid-campaign
- Not recognizing/rewarding top performers
